
## **The OpenClaw Moment: Revitalizing Web3 & Crypto**

**Wei Li (@0xWLi)**

### **Slide 1 — Title**

Hello everyone, I’m Wei Li.

For many years, decentralized social products have struggled. The vision was interesting, but the timing was not right. The infrastructure was incomplete, and the user experience was not strong enough.

My argument today is that this is beginning to change, because we are entering the era of **personal agents**.

### **Slide 2 — SocialFi Bottleneck**

A lot of SocialFi projects started from finance first and social second.

That created two problems: over-financialization, and the network-effect trap. People do not move to a new social platform just because it has tokens. They move when the interaction is better.

So the real challenge is not just ownership. It is building a better social coordination layer.

### **Slide 3 — Blockchain and AI**

I like to explain this with a simple phrase:

**Blockchain is the noun. AI is the verb.**

Blockchain gives us identity, ownership, and state. But those things are static.

AI gives us action. It can respond, coordinate, execute, and help users get things done.

Web3 has built strong nouns. What it has been missing is the verb.

### **Slide 4 — Personal Agents**

If you look at the history of computing, the PC made computing personal, and the mobile phone made it always with us.

I believe the next category is the **personal agent**.

Not just a chatbot in the cloud, but AI that represents the individual and can work on the individual’s behalf.

### **Slide 5 — The Internet Problem**

But today’s internet has a structural problem.

Most personal devices are behind NAT and firewalls. They are effectively unreachable. Even though we own our phones and laptops, they cannot easily act as independent nodes.

That is one reason the internet became so centralized.

### **Slide 6 — Decent Network**

This is where **Decent Network** comes in.

The idea is to build peer-to-peer connectivity around **identity**, not only around IP addresses.

That allows personal devices to become reachable participants in a decentralized mesh. Instead of being only clients of the cloud, they can become real nodes again.

### **Slide 7 — OpenClaw**

On top of that, we need an intelligence layer. That is where **OpenClaw** comes in.

OpenClaw treats agents as first-class entities with their own identity.

In the traditional model, bots depend on centralized platforms for accounts, permissions, and APIs. In OpenClaw, the agent’s address is its identity. It can exist and communicate more independently.

### **Slide 8 — The Stack**

So now we can finally describe a full stack.

**Layer 1** is the hardware: phones and computers.
**Layer 2** is the decentralized network layer: Decent Network.
**Layer 3** is the app and agent layer: Beagle and OpenClaw agents.

For the first time, the pieces fit together.

### **Slide 9 — Beagle Chat**

This is where **Beagle Chat** fits.

Beagle Chat is a decentralized, agent-native communication interface. Users, groups, and agents all have their own identities, and communication is designed for direct human-agent interaction.

The goal is not just another messenger. The goal is messaging for the personal-agent era.

### **Slide 10 — Beagle as an OpenClaw Channel**

One milestone I am especially excited about is that **Beagle now works as a native OpenClaw channel**.

That means users can talk directly with OpenClaw agents through Beagle.

This is important because it shows that agents do not have to live only inside centralized ecosystems like Telegram or Discord.

### **Slide 11 — Communities Become Alive**

This also changes what communities can be.

Instead of being passive chat rooms, communities can become active coordination systems.

Agents can summarize discussions, help newcomers, answer repeated questions, and assist with tasks. In that sense, they can act like digital employees or contributors.

### **Slide 12 — Conclusion**

So to conclude:

I believe the next wave of Web3 will not be driven by speculation alone. It will be driven by usable networks, personal agents, and communication systems that people can actually own.

That is the direction I am exploring with Decent Network, OpenClaw, and Beagle Chat.

Thank you.
